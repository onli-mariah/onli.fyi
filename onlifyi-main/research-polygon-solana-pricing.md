# Polygon and Solana Pricing Research - 2025

## Polygon (Layer 2 Solution)

### Transaction Costs
- **Typical range**: $0.0005 to $0.01 per transaction
- **Simple transfers**: Under $0.01 (often fractions of a cent)
- **Complex smart contracts**: Rarely exceed a few cents
- **Comparison to Ethereum**: 99% cheaper than Ethereum mainnet

### Gas Fee Structure
- Fees paid in POL (formerly MATIC)
- Denominated in Gwei
- Gas tracking available via Polygonscan

### Enterprise Considerations
- **NFT minting**: Can spike to higher fees during high demand, but still under $0.01 typically
- **DeFi operations**: Significantly cheaper than Ethereum mainnet
- **Predictability**: More stable than Ethereum, less volatile fee structure

### Sources
- Exolix.com (March 2025): Typical fees $0.0005-$0.01
- Bleap.finance (Sept 2025): Ethereum NFT mint can spike to $50+, Polygon remains under $0.01
- C-sharpcorner.com (Aug 2025): Fees range from fractions of a cent to a few cents
- Homecubes.io: Average gas fees $0.0005 to $0.01 per transaction

## Solana

### Transaction Costs (2025)
- **Average non-voting fee**: 0.000005 to 0.00007 SOL
- **USD equivalent** (at $170/SOL): $0.00085 to $0.0119
- **Typical range**: $0.0001 to $0.0025 USD
- **Base fee**: 5,000 lamports (~$0.0000075 at $150/SOL)
- **Often cited average**: $0.00025 per transaction

### Performance Metrics
- **Block time**: 400ms (0.4 seconds)
- **Transaction finality**: ~0.4 seconds (1 block)
- **Throughput**: 1,000+ user TPS (4,300+ including consensus votes)
- **Theoretical maximum**: 710,000 TPS on gigabit network

### Comparison to Other Blockchains (Finality Times)
- **Solana**: ~0.4 seconds, 1 block
- **Avalanche**: ~2 seconds, 1 block
- **Stellar**: ~5 seconds, 1 block
- **Ethereum**: ~3 minutes, 12 blocks
- **Polygon**: ~30 minutes, 372 blocks (for bridged USDC)

### Fee Predictability
- **Localized fee markets**: Only transactions accessing high-demand state see increased fees
- **No global fee market**: Unlike Ethereum, congestion is isolated
- **Failed transaction costs**: Ethereum users paid $150M+ for failed transactions during high demand periods; Solana avoids this

### Token Creation Costs
- **Typical range**: $50 to several hundred dollars
- **Much cheaper than Ethereum**: Ethereum basic ERC-20 costs $200-$400

### Enterprise Advantages
- Low and predictable fees
- Sub-second finality
- High throughput for volume operations
- State compression for cost-effective storage
- 1,300+ validator nodes in 35+ countries
