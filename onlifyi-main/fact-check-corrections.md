# Critical Discrepancies and Corrections from Fact-Check Report

## Major Errors Identified

### 1. Amazon Managed Blockchain - 100x Pricing Error
**Original Claim:** $30/hour for membership fees
**Actual Pricing:** $0.30/hour (Starter Edition) and $0.55/hour (Standard Edition)
**Impact:** 100x overstatement of Amazon infrastructure costs
**Correction Needed:** Realistic monthly infrastructure cost is approximately $1,400-$1,500/month (including Standard membership, two m5.2xlarge nodes, 1TB storage), not the inflated figures implied

### 2. Ethereum Gas Costs - Severely Outdated (28x Higher)
**Original Claim:** $0.28 per transaction (low gas scenario)
**Current Reality (Dec 2025):** <$0.01 (0.032 Gwei)
**Historical Context:** Claims based on 2023-early 2024 data, before Dencun upgrade (March 2024)
**Impact:** After Dencun upgrade and Layer-2 migration, gas prices collapsed by over 95%
**Current Layer-2 Solutions:** Polygon, Arbitrum, Optimism now offer transactions under $0.01

**Corrected Gas Scenarios:**
- Current low: <$0.01 (not $0.28 - 28x overstatement)
- Moderate congestion: $0.20-$1.00 (not $5.00)
- Historical peaks: $70-$145 (accurate for Feb 2024 DeFi/NFT surges)

### 3. Oracle Transaction Fee - Misleading Claim
**Original Claim:** $0.75 per transaction as an Oracle fee
**Actual Reality:** Oracle Blockchain Platform (Hyperledger Fabric) has ZERO protocol transaction fees
**Clarification:** The $0.75 figure represents an estimated operational cost per transaction (infrastructure expenses divided by transaction volume), not an Oracle-imposed fee

**Oracle Digital Assets Edition:** Includes pre-built NFT marketplace chaincode templates, potentially reducing development costs to $300K-$800K (not the claimed $1.5M for raw infrastructure builds)

### 4. Onli Pricing Discrepancies
**Treasury Deployment:**
- Original claim: $5,000
- Website shows: $0.00005 per asset deployment
- Status: NOT FOUND in documentation

**Genome Issuance:**
- Original claim: $0.50 per genome
- Website shows: $0.05 per asset (10x difference)
- Status: DISCREPANCY

**Technical Claims:**
- "Hyperdimensional vector storage" - Marketing term, not found in computer science literature
- "EVD process" - Zero search results, NOT VERIFIED
- **Critical Issue:** Zero third-party validation found (no independent reviews, analyst coverage, customer testimonials, or industry recognition outside Onli's own materials)

### 5. Missing Competitive Alternatives

**Major Layer-2 Platforms Omitted:**
- **Polygon:** Powers Nike's .SWOOSH ($185M+ NFT revenue), Starbucks Odyssey; near-zero gas fees, Ethereum-compatible
- **Immutable X:** Zero gas fees for minting/trading, powers GameStop marketplace, ZK-rollup technology
- **Flow:** Built by Dapper Labs, powers NBA Top Shot and NFL ALL DAY; $725M ecosystem fund
- **Solana:** 50,000 TPS, ~$0.00025 transaction costs, growing gaming ecosystem

**White-Label Solutions:**
- TurnkeyTown: 2-3 weeks deployment
- Blockchain App Factory: 48 hours deployment
- 4IRE: 3 weeks, ~$20,000+
- Innowise: Days-weeks, subscription model

**Real-World Enterprise Choices:** Nike, Starbucks, and Adidas chose Polygon-based solutions rather than Oracle/Amazon/Ethereum mainnet

### 6. Polygon Context - NFT-Specific Solutions
**Important Note:** Polygon and similar Layer-2 solutions mentioned are primarily NFT marketplace solutions. While technically successful, many NFT-specific marketplaces have faced challenges:
- Market volatility and declining NFT trading volumes post-2022
- Concentration in specific use cases (gaming, collectibles, brand loyalty)
- Not necessarily representative of broader enterprise digital asset needs

The distinction between "infrastructure vs. application" is valid and well-articulated. Blockchain platforms do provide infrastructure while organizations must build complete applications.

## Systematic Bias Assessment

**The fact-check reveals systematic bias:**
1. Blockchain platform costs appear inflated through errors and outdated data
2. Onli costs appear optimized
3. Amazon 100x pricing error fundamentally distorts TCO comparison
4. Outdated Ethereum gas data makes Ethereum appear artificially unfavorable
5. Missing competitive alternatives (Polygon, Immutable X, Flow, Solana, white-label solutions)

## Recommendations for Corrected Analysis

1. **Fix Amazon Pricing:** Use actual $0.30/hour membership fee, realistic $1,400-$1,500/month infrastructure costs
2. **Update Ethereum Gas Costs:** Use current December 2025 data (<$0.01 for Layer-2)
3. **Add Layer-2 Comparison:** Include Polygon, Arbitrum, Optimism as separate competitive options
4. **Clarify Oracle Fees:** Distinguish between protocol fees (zero) and operational cost estimates
5. **Add Polygon/Layer-2 Context:** Note that these are primarily NFT solutions with mixed market success
6. **Include White-Label Options:** Show dramatically lower entry costs and faster deployment times
7. **Add Caveats for Onli:** Note limited third-party validation and pricing discrepancies
8. **Verify Current Pricing:** Obtain direct vendor quotes for all platforms (December 2025)
9. **Architectural Trade-offs:** Discuss when decentralization, public audit trails, and ecosystem interoperability matter vs. operational simplicity

## Key Takeaway

The foundational insight about infrastructure vs. application development is accurate and valuable. The $225K-$1.5M development cost range is well-supported. However, the specific TCO calculations cannot be considered reliable due to the Amazon 100x error, severely outdated Ethereum data, and incomplete competitive analysis. Organizations should verify current pricing directly with vendors and consider Layer-2 alternatives that fundamentally change the cost equation.
