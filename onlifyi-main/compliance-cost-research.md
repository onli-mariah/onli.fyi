# Compliance Cost Research Findings

## Source: Flagright - "Overcoming the Hidden Costs of AML Compliance"
**URL:** https://www.flagright.com/post/overcoming-the-hidden-costs-of-aml-compliance
**Date:** June 30, 2025

### Key Findings

**Global AML Compliance Spending:**
- **$206 billion per year** globally spent on financial crime compliance by fintechs and banks
- **98% of institutions** reported compliance costs increased in 2023 vs prior year
- **EMEA region alone:** $85 billion spent on AML efforts in 2023
- **Average compliance cost:** ~19% of a financial firm's annual revenue

### Hidden Cost Categories

#### 1. Operational Inefficiencies
- Manual reviews, spreadsheet reconciliation, swivel-chair workflows
- Separate systems for transaction monitoring, sanctions screening, case management, KYC
- Data silos and outdated legacy systems create avoidable expense
- **Impact:** Slow processes, high error rates, missed red flags, employee burnout

**Specific Costs:**
- Alert investigation process without automation: hours per alert vs minutes
- Teams "throw people at the problem" instead of fixing root cause
- Higher staffing costs to compensate for inefficiency

#### 2. False Positives and Alert Fatigue
- Rules-based transaction monitoring systems are "notoriously noisy"
- **4 hours average** to investigate each alert at one global bank
- 90% of alerts prove to be false positives
- Alert fatigue leads to desensitization and missed real threats

**Impact:**
- Wasted investigation resources
- Slower response to real threats
- Reputational risk from flagging legitimate customer transactions
- Customer frustration and lost trust

#### 3. Rising Staffing Costs
- **61% increase** in employee hours spent on compliance (2016-2023)
- IT spending on compliance rose from **9.6% to 13.4%** of IT budgets
- Some global banks **quadrupled compliance staff** (1,500 to 6,000 employees)
- Compliance teams now rival front-office business units in size

**Why this happens:**
- Firms pay for extra headcount to compensate for suboptimal tech/workflows
- Manual labor is expensive (salaries, training, overhead) and still error-prone
- Over-reliance on people instead of automation

### Implications for Blockchain Marketplaces

Traditional blockchain/crypto marketplaces must build and maintain:
1. KYC/AML integration with third-party providers
2. Transaction monitoring systems
3. Sanctions screening infrastructure
4. Case management systems
5. Compliance teams (analysts, investigators, officers)
6. Ongoing regulatory reporting systems

**Estimated Costs for Blockchain Marketplace:**
- Initial KYC/AML integration: $50K-$200K
- Ongoing KYC/AML services: $2K-$10K/month ($24K-$120K/year)
- Compliance officer salaries: $120K-$180K/year
- Compliance team (2-4 analysts): $240K-$720K/year
- Regulatory legal counsel: $100K-$500K/year
- Audit and reporting systems: $50K-$150K initial
- **Total annual compliance costs:** $500K-$1.5M+ for mid-size marketplace
