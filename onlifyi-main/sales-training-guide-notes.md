## Audience-Specific Pain Points

### Enterprises & Corporations
**Pain Points:**
- Custody risk
- Lack of regulatory clarity
- Can't recognize assets on balance sheets
- High costs
- Specialized talent shortage

**Core Message:** "Onli provides balance-sheet-ready digital property with 91-98% cost savings, zero custody risk, and built-in regulatory compliance—enabling institutional adoption that blockchain cannot achieve."

### Developers & Technical Teams
**Pain Points:**
- Steep learning curve (Solidity, Rust)
- High and volatile gas fees
- Slow transaction times
- Security vulnerabilities (smart contracts)
- Custody burden

**Core Message:** "Build digital asset applications in 2-3 months with zero transaction fees, simple APIs, and no blockchain expertise required—so you can focus on user experience, not infrastructure."

### End Users & Consumers
**Pain Points:**
- Fear of scams
- Permanent loss of keys
- Lack of privacy
- High transaction fees
- Confusing UX

**Core Message:** "Own true digital property with provable possession, instant transfers, and zero fees—it works like your favorite payment app, but you actually own the asset."

## Key Technical Differentiators

1. **Actual Possession vs Records:** Onli gives you the asset itself, not just a ledger entry
2. **Hardware-Enforced Security:** Vaults use TEE (Intel SGX/ARM TrustZone) - physical guarantee
3. **Use Policies:** Declarative rules (non-Turing-complete) replace vulnerable smart contracts
4. **EVD Protocol:** Evolve-Validate-Delete prevents double-spending without consensus
5. **No Consensus Overhead:** Eliminates the "architectural tax" of blockchain

## Objection Handling

**"Sounds centralized"**
- Onli is distributed, not centralized
- Doesn't require decentralized consensus
- Peer-to-peer architecture, censorship-resistant

**"How is this different from a database?"**
- Super-user problem: Database admins can copy/edit/delete
- Onli uses hardware Vaults where NO ONE (not even Onli) can access
- Physical guarantee vs software promise

**"What about smart contracts?"**
- Smart contracts = $4.4B in losses from hacks
- Onli uses declarative Use Policies (clear rules, not complex code)
- Eliminates attack surface

**"Double-spending without blockchain?"**
- EVD protocol: asset cryptographically evolves and moves
- Hardware-enforced provable deletion
- Atomic finality without consensus
